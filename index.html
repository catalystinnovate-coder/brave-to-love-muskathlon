What is This

More Than
a Race.

In 2026, Brave to Love and Muskathlon are joining forces for a week-long endurance event at Roaring Sky Base Camp, South Africa. Participants from around the world will run, walk, and cycle — not just to finish a race, but to raise funds that will build a safe house for survivors of abuse and exploitation.

This is your chance to be part of something that outlasts the finish line. Every kilometre you cover. Every rand raised. Every person who hears about this initiative. It all counts.

"

An adventure that fights injustice — and builds a future for survivors.

The Muskathlon model combines endurance sport, community, and direct charitable impact. Participants raise funds that go entirely to the cause — in this case, a safe house for Brave to Love's survivors.

Brave to Love

The Cause

Building a
Safe House.

Brave to Love works with survivors of trafficking, abuse, and exploitation — people who have experienced some of the deepest injustice imaginable. Their work doesn't stop at rescue. They walk alongside survivors through rehabilitation, skills development, and economic independence — equipping each person to reintegrate safely and confidently into society.

Right now, there is a critical need for a dedicated safe house: a place of refuge, recovery, and real transformation — where survivors can begin to rebuild their lives in safety.

This Muskathlon event is the vehicle. Every participant who registers, every rand raised by their network, every social media post — all of it contributes directly to making that safe house a reality.

You may not be able to walk in someone's shoes. But you can run to change their future.

Raise More, Build More

How to
fundraise.

You don't need a huge following. You just need to share something real. Here's how participants have successfully raised funds for Muskathlon events around the world.

1

Tell the Story

Share Brave to Love's mission and why you're running. People give to causes they understand — and to people they trust. Explain what the safe house means in plain, honest terms.

2

Document Your Training

Post your preparation. Your early morning runs, your tired legs, your progress. People want to follow a journey — make them part of yours and direct them to your fundraising profile.

3

Ask Directly

Don't just post and hope. Send personal messages. Ask colleagues, family, your church or community. A direct ask converts far better than a general social media post.

4

Set a Milestone Goal

Break your target into milestones and celebrate publicly when you hit them. "I'm at 50% — help me get to 75%!" creates urgency and gives people a reason to share your page.

About
Muskathlon.

Muskathlon is a global movement that combines world-class endurance events with on-the-ground exposure to injustice and direct charitable funding. Every event takes place in a location where the charity partner operates — making the impact personal, visible, and real.

Participants raise significant funds that go directly to local NGO partners — in this case, 100% to Brave to Love's safe house project.

Each participant gets a personal fundraising page on the Muskathlon platform — a purpose-built tool for reaching their network and tracking progress.

The event is run, walked, or cycled — making it accessible to people of different fitness levels and athletic backgrounds.

Muskathlons have taken place across multiple continents, raising millions for justice-focused organisations around the world.
